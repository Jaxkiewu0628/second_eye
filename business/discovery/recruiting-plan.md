# Second Eye — Discovery Recruiting Plan

_Goal: line up ~10 blind/low-vision users, 3 O&M instructors, and 2 funders (VA + voc-rehab) for 30-minute calls. Free or near-free. Target: booked within ~1 week._

## Who we need (and the mix)
| Segment | Count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Totally blind, cane users | 3–4 | Core fit for an audio/no-display device |
| Totally blind, guide-dog users | 2 | Strong, different views on wearables |
| Low-vision (some usable sight) | 3 | The bigger population; tests the "no display" limit |
| O&M instructors | 3 | See patterns across many travelers |
| VA VIST coordinator | 1 | The most concentrated payer |
| State voc-rehab counselor | 1 | The other real funding channel |

Spread age and tech-comfort on purpose. Note each person's segment — the wedge answer may differ by group.

## Channels (fastest first)
1. **Reddit r/Blind** — active, candid. One honest "I'm researching, not selling" post. Highest volume.
2. **AppleVis forums** — tech-savvy blind users who try assistive apps. Great for the free-app comparison.
3. **Local NFB and ACB chapters** (National Federation of the Blind / American Council of the Blind) — email the chapter president; they often connect researchers to members.
4. **A nearby low-vision clinic or vision-rehab center** — front desk or the O&M department.
5. **University O&M / vision-rehab programs** — instructors and faculty; also reach BROS/CVRT professionals.
6. **VA VIST coordinator** — every VA medical center has one for blind/low-vision veterans.
7. **Blind creators** — YouTubers/podcasters in the community (e.g., AppleVis contributors, blind tech reviewers); a short respectful DM.

## Ethics + comp
- Be transparent: you're a founder researching, nothing to buy.
- Offer a $25–40 gift card for 30 minutes (respect their time; it also improves show-rate). O&M instructors may decline comp — offer anyway.
- Don't over-promise a product or a timeline. Ask permission to record. Make it easy to decline.

## Outreach templates

**r/Blind post**
> Title: Researching everyday travel + reading friction — would love 30 min (not selling anything)
> Hi all. I'm working on assistive tech and, before building anything, I want to understand how you actually get around and get information day to day, and where the real friction is. Not selling, no pitch. 30-minute call, your choice of voice/video/text. Happy to offer a small thank-you gift card. If you're open to it, comment or DM. Thank you.

**Email — NFB/ACB chapter**
> Subject: 30-min interviews with members — early assistive-tech research
> Hi [name], I'm a founder researching everyday mobility and reading challenges for blind and low-vision people, before designing anything. I'd be grateful to speak with a few members for 30 minutes (small thank-you gift card offered). I'm not selling and won't pitch — I'm trying to learn what actually helps. Could you point me to anyone open to a short call? Thank you for the work your chapter does.

**Email — O&M instructor**
> Subject: 30 min with an O&M instructor — understanding travel friction
> Hi [name], you watch far more blind travelers than any single user can describe. I'm researching where mobility and information-access tools fall short in real life, before building anything. Could I get 30 minutes of your perspective? Not a sales call. Happy to work around your schedule.

**Email — VA VIST coordinator**
> Subject: Quick question on how blind veterans get assistive devices
> Hi [name], I'm researching assistive technology for blind and low-vision people. I'd value 15–20 minutes to understand how devices (like electronic mobility/reading aids) get evaluated and funded for veterans through the VA, and what tends to actually get adopted. Not selling anything. Thank you for your time.

## Timeline
- **Day 1–2:** post to r/Blind + AppleVis; email 2 chapters, 1 clinic, 2 O&M programs, VA VIST, voc-rehab.
- **Day 3–7:** book and run calls as they come in. Aim 2–3/day.
- **After ~10 user calls:** tally the mobility-vs-reading picks (see interview guide), decide the wedge.

## 结论 / verdict
**可行性:** CONDITIONAL (lean toward NOT now for the core team). The technology transfers cleanly and there IS a genuine, adversarially-confirmed seam — infrastructure-free, egocentric, on-device-offline vision that sees UN-tagged hazards and warns the worker, at an individual price the category structurally doesn't offer — that no shipped product occupies. BUT it is a NARROW combination seam inside a mature, well-capitalized RED ocean, not open blue water, and three facts cap it: (1) on our most-painful case (struck-by), pure wearable vision only warns one side while UWB warns both — a structural weakness; (2) the buyer/GTM (enterprise EHS/insurer, 6–12mo procurement, workers'-comp ROI, certification) is a DIFFERENT company from our funded blind-user grants/VA/direct path; (3) the deepest-pain verticals (mining, Ex oil/gas) are gated by intrinsic-safety cert we've never done. Feasible technically; feasibility as a business for a two-person team RIGHT NOW is the real constraint.

**推荐:** Do NOT pivot or fork the team into worker-safety now — protect the funded blind-mobility core (that is where the payer path, physics de-risking, and current momentum live). Treat worker-safety as VALIDATED OPTIONALITY / a V2+ migration story for the fundraise narrative, not a second product line. Concretely: (1) Keep architecting the engine to be hazard-class-agnostic (blind curbs and worker edges are the same primitive) so a worker SKU is a model+ruggedization delta, not a rebuild — the V1 downward-ToF drop-off work already does double duty. (2) If — and only if — you probe worker-safety, run ONE cheap discovery probe into the single best-fit wedge: infrastructure-free personal fall/edge/vehicle-proximity vision for NON-Ex outdoor lone/roaming field & O&M work (utility line, water/wastewater, solar/wind O&M, laydown yards, roadwork) sold as a COMPLEMENTARY worker-worn layer, and pitch it on insurance/workers'-comp ROI + privacy-safe (offline, alert-only, no tracking) — NOT on hardware specs. (3) Hard NO to: fall/IMU me-too, UWB proximity, certified machine-stop, gas/lone-worker pucks, fixed-CCTV CV platforms, warehouse-fixed forklift CV, and the Ex core. Gating risks to resolve before treating this as more than optionality: the one-sided struck-by weakness, who actually pays, and whether privacy-offline is enough of a wedge to justify a second GTM.

## 我们的能力契合 / capability fit

Our four-legged playbook (cheap ~$141 BOM / ~30g wearable / on-device OFFLINE camera+ToF+YOLO / data flywheel) fits best precisely where the environment is unstructured, off-grid, mobile, and un-instrumented — the common blind spot of the entire field. Every incumbent is one of: (A) fixed/machine-POV CV bolted to ceiling or vehicle, (B) a dumb radio tag that only senses PRE-TAGGED objects and is blind to untagged hazards, or (C) a cloud/telemetry puck that streams to a supervisor. Our unique standing capability is the ONLY one that is worker-POV + sees un-instrumented hazards + needs zero site setup + alerts the wearer (not a dashboard) + runs with no connectivity. Strongest concrete jobs: fall-from-height / drop-off / edge detection (downward ToF, already in V1) and untagged-vehicle/equipment proximity in yards. Honest limits: (1) on-device egocentric CV is NOT a moat by itself (Chooch/Guardhat/viAct do edge inference; academic YOLO-on-helmet is everywhere) — the moat is the cheap+offline+untagged-vision+individual-price COMBINATION plus the flywheel and execution/reliability; (2) camera degrades in dust/smoke/dark where RF wins — position as a complementary layer, not a replacement.

## 最有护城河机会 / top opportunities

### Non-Ex outdoor field / O&M lone-and-roaming work (utility line/field, water/wastewater, solar & wind O&M, construction laydown yards, road-work zones)
- **为什么:** This is where ALL FOUR of our properties hold at once and no incumbent reaches: UWB/RTLS is uneconomic on transient equipment and mobile crews (needs anchors on every vehicle+zone), fixed CV can't cover roaming/occluded/off-grid workers, and connected-safety pucks (Blackline) are cloud-by-design so they die where connectivity dies. No intrinsic-safety wall (non-Ex), so our $141 cost moat survives. Fall-from-height + untagged-vehicle proximity are the exact jobs our V1 stack already does (downward-ToF drop-off detection was pulled into V1).
- **护城河角度:** Infrastructure-ZERO egocentric on-device-offline vision: works the moment one worker clips it on, anywhere, no beacons/gateways/cloud, sees UN-tagged hazards (edges, holes, untagged vehicles, swinging loads) a radio tag is structurally blind to. Privacy-safe (no cloud, no upload, alerts only the wearer) directly answers the 46%/59% worker resistance to biometrics/tracking that every cloud incumbent trips on. Data flywheel on real worker-POV near-miss footage compounds and tag-based systems structurally cannot produce it.
### Small / mid contractors and temp / contract labor priced out of enterprise safety platforms
- **为什么:** Cost is the repeatedly-cited #1 adoption barrier for small contractors; the entire category hides price behind enterprise SaaS / insurance-bundling / multi-year leases with no simple affordable buy-once individual device. Our BOM enables a price point the category structurally does not offer, and single-worker zero-capex deploy fits crews too small/transient for Kwant-class rollouts.
- **护城河角度:** Individual-priced, buy-once, per-worker personal protection with no per-seat subscription and no site instrumentation — an economic segment the incumbents' capital+SaaS model cannot serve.

## 红海别碰 / avoid

DO NOT touch, in priority order: (1) Fall/man-down detection via IMU — commodity, cheap, owned by Blackline/Kwant/Spot-r; pure me-too, violates iron rule 1. (2) UWB/RFID vehicle-pedestrian PROXIMITY as a category — owned by ELOKON/Litum/Pozyx/ZoneSafe/Reactec; they warn BOTH sides and see through dust/smoke/no-line-of-sight where our camera fails; do not go head-to-head. (3) Certified machine-guarding / machine-STOP (ISO 13849/SIL, Level-9 PDS) — SICK/Pilz/Keyence/Omron/Matrix/Newtrax; we legally cannot be the certified stop function. (4) Connected gas-detection + lone-worker telemetry — Blackline ($850M take-private) / Honeywell / MSA / Dräger; a me-too cloud safety puck is suicide. (5) Cloud AI-vision safety PLATFORMS on fixed CCTV for PPE/compliance dashboards — Intenseye ($64M), Protex ($36M), Voxel, Newmetrix/Oracle. (6) Fixed high-throughput warehouse forklift CV — ELOKON/Proxicam plus $699 Shenzhen commodity that erases our cost edge. (7) The classified Ex core of oil/gas/chem/mining — intrinsic-safety cert wall ($5–6.5k IS cameras) evaporates our cost moat exactly where the hazard is.

## B2B 门槛 / barriers

HIGH and structurally different from our blind-user path. (1) Certification: OSHA/CE baseline is manageable, but any Ex environment (oil/gas/mining/chem) demands ATEX/IECEx/MSHA intrinsic-safety — Zone-1 is 40–60% costlier, months of scarce third-party testing, and certified IS cameras run $5–6.5k, wiping out our cost moat; this both gates and moats. Strategy: enter only NON-Ex scenes to avoid/defer it. (2) Procurement: enterprise B2B cycle 6–12 months, 5–10 decision-makers, +4–8 weeks safety/security review, 9–18 month platform deployments — hostile to a two-person startup. (3) Payer: the buyer is the EHS manager / GC / insurer, NOT the worker; budget is truly unlocked by workers'-comp ROI (early vendor data cites 50–64% injury-rate drop, 58% claims-cost drop, 250%+ ROI — but these are vendor/manufacturing figures, not independent long-term construction studies, so they need our own pilot to substantiate). (4) Adoption/privacy: 46% of workers resist biometrics, 59% resist location tracking, unions constrain any monitoring — a wall for cloud incumbents that our offline/alert-only/no-upload architecture turns into an ADVANTAGE. (5) Legal ceiling: in machine/mine contexts we can only be advisory PPE, never the certified stop.

## 和盲人的协同 / synergy

STRONG — same engine, different label. The core is identical: camera + ToF depth + on-device YOLO + bone-conduction/haptic 'hazard ahead,' fully offline, ~$141/~30g, plus the data-flywheel loop. Blind mobility already validates and de-risks the hard physics (outdoor obstacle/curb/drop-off detection on a thin frame + ToF), and the drop-off/edge detection just pulled into V1 is the same primitive worker fall-from-height needs. A worker build reuses ~90% of the perception stack — the deltas are the hazard class model (forklift/edge/machine vs curb/pole/branch), ruggedization, and a different alert taxonomy. The genuine DIVERGENCE is business, not tech: blind = individual buyer + grants/VA/voc-rehab reimbursement + direct sales; worker = enterprise EHS/insurer buyer + long B2B procurement + certification. So one engine can serve both, but they are effectively two companies on GTM, and splitting a two-person team across both would dilute the funded core. Best used as an optionality/V2+ migration story, not a now-fork.

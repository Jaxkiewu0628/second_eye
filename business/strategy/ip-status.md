# Second Eye — IP / Ownership Status

_2026-06-29. Practical CEO read, not legal advice. Confirm with an IP lawyer before any raise or patent filing._

## Bottom line: not a blocker. A couple of emails and a clean incorporation.

## What's settled
- **Conrad: Jackie owns YCane.** The Conrad Challenge Terms & Conditions state submissions remain the participant's IP. Conrad / Space Center Houston hold only a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to *show off* the submission (publish, display, promo). That is a bragging-rights license, not an ownership grab. It does not block commercialization. Source: https://www.conradchallenge.org/terms-conditions
- **The real de-risker: Second Eye is new tech.** The 2023 YCane was a high-school *concept* (solar, bone-conduction pitch) with no working AI. The company's actual asset is what Jackie built *after* the competition, on his own: the validated on-device detector, the offline voice loop, the RV1106 work, with his own commit history. Ideas aren't ownable; implementations are. Old YCane = inspiration / prior art, not the product.

## Two cheap cleanups before raising or filing
1. **WFLA (Jackie's Shanghai international high school, where YCane was made):** high schools rarely claim student IP, but policies vary, especially if school labs, funding, or a teacher-mentor materially contributed. Check the student handbook or send one email. Probably clean.
2. **Original teammates:** Jackie was the only *tech* person, but others were on the *pitch/business* side. Co-creators of the 2023 submission could in theory claim a piece of that old work. Get a one-paragraph written acknowledgment that they have no claim on the new venture.

## Recommendations
- **Drop the "YCane" name; launch as "Second Eye."** Clean break from the old submission's promo license and old teammates, and a better brand.
- **At incorporation, do a proper US founder IP-assignment** so all of Jackie's tech lands in NewCo. China-origin work + now-at-WashU is fine; just assign cleanly at formation.
- **One hour with an IP lawyer** before any raise or patent. Cheap insurance.
